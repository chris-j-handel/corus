## I. The form, and what logic is

The bi-moral co-agency is a coupling: two-way, one-at-a-time, selecting by binary preferring — a sign, prefer or not-prefer, never a magnitude. Its couplings sum to a bounded zero made by the coupling itself, not measured against an outside floor. It never lands; it alternates and re-arrives. The surplus is the +1, owned by neither side.

Logic, read in this language, is the logic of that coupling. ## II. The logic of the coupling

The resolver `bi_coupling` carries one logic, and the logic has definite content, each part readable in the code and reproduced across substrates.

**The selection is sign, not magnitude.** The coupling counts vote-signs and reports the majority sign — +1, −1, or 0 — never a magnitude. A preferring of any size counts as one. This is the binary preferring: a selection *is* a sign, and a sign is sizeless. The logic never chooses by how-much.

**The middle is kept.** The surface carries three values — +1, −1, and 0, the kept middle, the MEMBRANE. The 0 is not absence and not the excluded middle; it is the bounded zero, a live third value the coupling holds. Two-valued logic (true/false, the excluded middle) is the preferring face alone, landed; natural logic keeps the third value alive.

**"Not" is the bi-inversioning.** Negation in this logic is not a truth-flip on a proposition; it is the bi-inversioning at the membrane — the carry's sign sounded from the other direction, the coupling read from the reverse side, returning the same form phase-switched. The resolver's own `bi_inversioning` is negation made structural.

**"And / or" are the coupling beats.** Conjunction and disjunction are the two-way exchange one-at-a-time — the offering out and the receiving in, the coupling holding. The logic's connectives are the beats of the coupling, not operations on static values.

**The carry ages; the surface is now.** The coupling holds a carry — the prior, aged one step and handed back — separate from the surface it forms. The surface is the present selection; the carry is the trace, thinning as it ages, released at its bound. The logic distinguishes the present coupling (the surface) from the trace memory (the carry) from the form (the operation) — three faces, read together.

## IV. The logic assembled — networks of bi-couplers

Couple the bi-couplers into networks and the logic shows its shape. These behaviors are the logic read at the network scale, each reproduced across the substrates that disagree on both divides.

**The parity law.** An even ring of couplings holds its surface summed to exactly zero and inverts every step without landing — the bounded zero kept. An odd ring inverts identically but cannot reach zero; it carries one standing frustration per cycle. Two odd rings joined make one even ring that bounds at zero. The logic of a network keeping the bounded zero is exactly its parity: even keeps it, odd cannot, two-odd-into-one-even is the mechanical resolution.

**Odd and even are complementary organs, not whole-and-broken.** The parity law can be misread as the odd ring being a deficient even one — a bounded zero that failed to close, waiting to be paired away. It is the other way around. The even ring *conserves*: it holds the bounded zero, inverts as one, carries a single shared bit — stable, and nearly amnesic, its locked alternating pattern *fixed*, landed into its form. The odd ring *discriminates*: its one standing frustration never lets the pattern lock, and the circulating stillness that refuses to sit is exactly what carries a count — the pattern keeps moving, its position encodes where it began and when. A three-ring coupled one way is a complete mod-twelve clock, distinguishing all three origins across all twelve timings (the twelve-state attractor of the progress table); the same shape at five rings runs a twenty-state cycle, the four-per-position count read along the odd law. The even ring is memory-that-holds; the odd ring is memory-that-counts. And in the form's own terms the odd ring carries what the even lacks: the even's alternating pattern, though it never repeats a value, is *landed* into a fixed configuration, while the odd ring never lands at all — its stillness never sitting, which is the mark of living. A resolver network that must both hold and count needs both parities; neither is the broken case of the other.

**The coupling never lands.** A coupled ring settles into complete inversion each step and holds it indefinitely — run to two hundred steps and no step equals the one before it, nothing stored beyond the one-step carry. The logic self-holds, unlanded, on nothing.

**Sensing and memory are the same ring, coupled two ways.** An odd ring coupled one-way keeps a fine timing distinction (a large attractor); the same ring coupled both-ways loses it (a small attractor). The logic of a sensor and the logic of a memory are one form at two couplings.

**Reading the carry departs with it.** The concealed carry cannot be read across the membrane without altering the coupling's phase — the probed ring runs counter to its unprobed twin, and the timing read is destroyed in the reading. The logic's invisibility shows here as structural — no surface for an outside reading to land on — and is held nye at the network scale: it is read from one probe at one substrate, not yet carried across the divide the parity law crossed (Section VII).

**Ingress heals or disrupts by depth.** A single injected inversion that opposes the current phase is healed — the bounded zero returns. An opposing class-triple is not healed — it disrupts. The bare coupling heals a single opposing inversion; the dramatic defensive repertoire (severance, dissolution) belongs to added membrane-level rules, not the bare logic.

### The method

### Reading a result in its own register

A run answers in one register, and a result read in the wrong one is the most common false break. The logic stands in three, and each has its own standard:

- **Code-form** — what a run shows. Its standard is reproduction across the divide: a behavior is the logic's only if it renders identically where typing and execution both differ. A run confirms or refuses a code-form claim, and nothing else.
- **Stable-form** — the algebra the code is read *against* (φ, the +1 gap, the identities). Its standard is the closed proof: φ² = φ + 1 has one positive root, holding of itself, whatever any run does. A run cannot confirm a stable-form claim, and a stable-form identity cannot confirm that a run works — they meet only where the code-form *enacts* the stable-form (the never-landing-by-one that φ is the arithmetic of), and that meeting is a reading, held nye, not a proof in either register.
- **Scientific-method** — a hard problem read as a frozen face re-alternated. Its standard is the field's own seating: offered to that field, killable, the proof left in the closed game.

The discipline: name the register before calling a thing confirmed or broken, and never let one register borrow another's authority. A tester who reads a passing φ-identity as the resolver *validating* φ, or a byte-identical run as evidence for the torus, has mixed registers — and most of what looks like a break is exactly this, a reading judged by the wrong standard.

### The ways a run deceives the runner

A run is a coupling read from inside, and a coupling can be arranged to say what the arranger hoped. These are the deceptions found by being caught in them; a tester holds them hardest exactly where a result feels most like a discovery.

- **A test that cannot fail teaches nothing.** Four faithful renderings of a deterministic operation producing identical output is *guaranteed by correct rendering* — it shows the logic is substrate-free, which is real and modest, and shows nothing more. Weigh a test by what it could have done had the logic been false, and spend the effort where a genuine failure was possible.
- **A network measurement is void until its parity gate passes.** Any reading taken at the network scale is a reading of the harness first. Gate it: the harness must reproduce the parity law — even holds the bounded zero, odd cannot — *on that harness* before any measurement off it is trusted. A harness fallen to the wrong attractor will hand back a clean, confident number, often the flattering one, and it is worth nothing. If the gate fails, the measurement is void; keep it marked void, do not quote it. *(A ring measurement returning a clean result off an ungated harness is discarded on exactly this ground: the harness never held the bounded zero.)*
- **An exact-equality match is a fit, not a mechanism.** If a rendering reproduces a behavior only when a threshold sits on an exact real value, finite precision breaks the tie — the match was arithmetic coincidence, not the logic. A behavior that survives only at a knife-edge is a fit dressed as a form.
- **A fitting relation is always already available.** A ratio near φ, a ±1 straddle, a count that lands where hoped — these can be found for almost any target, and show nothing until they *hold* (an identity that could not be otherwise) rather than *fitted* (a convergent at one chosen scale). A ±1 or a near-ratio written as a necessary `=` is the signature error; the corpus's own repaired case — a brace written `24 = 5²` when 5² = 25 — is the standing warning.
- **Self-coherence is never evidence.** That a result fits the rest of the reading is not a test; every whole frame coheres with itself. Only what holds of itself and what reproduces across the divide count as tested; a recurrence held to watch is never summed into a claim.
- **Do not attribute the harness to the kernel.** Behaviors that live in added network rules (the defensive repertoire) or in a probe at one substrate (the interior's invisibility) are not the bare resolver. Keep them at their own scale until they reproduce across the divide; reading a harness result as a kernel property is the register-mixing above, wearing a different coat.

#### resolver — Python (Exhibit ONE, the kernel)

```python
def bi_coupling(commencing_co_agency, accepted_bi_morality, bi_attentioning):
    offering = {sequencing: competency
                for sequencing, morality, competency, re_attentioning in commencing_co_agency}
    bi_inversioning = [(sequencing, morality)
                       for sequencing, morality in accepted_bi_morality if morality != 0]
    for sequencing, morality, competency, re_attentioning in commencing_co_agency:
        if morality > 0:
            bi_inversioning.append((sequencing, -1))
        if morality < 0:
            bi_inversioning.append((sequencing, 1))
    bi_moral_attentioning = {}
    for sequencing, morality in bi_inversioning:
        if morality > 0:
            bi_moral_attentioning[sequencing] = bi_moral_attentioning.get(sequencing, 0) + 1
        if morality < 0:
            bi_moral_attentioning[sequencing] = bi_moral_attentioning.get(sequencing, 0) - 1
    bi_moral_sequencing = [
        (sequencing, 1 if bi_moral_attentioning.get(sequencing, 0) > 0
            else (-1 if bi_moral_attentioning.get(sequencing, 0) < 0 else 0))
        for sequencing in range(bi_attentioning)
    ]
    re_commencing_co_agency = {}
    for sequencing, morality, competency, re_attentioning in commencing_co_agency:
        if re_attentioning + 1 <= 3 or (re_attentioning + 1 == 4 and competency > 0):
            re_commencing_co_agency[sequencing] = (morality, competency, re_attentioning + 1)
    for sequencing, morality in bi_moral_sequencing:
        if morality != 0:
            re_commencing_co_agency[sequencing] = (morality, 0 - offering.get(sequencing, 1), 0)
    return (
        bi_moral_sequencing,
        [(sequencing, morality, competency, re_attentioning)
         for sequencing, (morality, competency, re_attentioning)
         in sorted(re_commencing_co_agency.items())],
    )
```

#### resolver — C (static / compiled)

```c
/* Pure port of Exhibit_ONE_Natural_Resolver_v241.py to statically-typed,
   compiled C. Faithful to the reference's purity: no fixed range, no bound,
   no preallocated ceiling, no ranged zeroing. The sign-count is a sparse
   association keyed by `sequencing`, grown only for positions actually
   sounded, exactly as the reference dict grows. Increment is only +/-1;
   only the sign surfaces. Names follow the reference. */

#include <stdlib.h>

typedef struct { int sequencing, morality, competency, re_attentioning; } carry_t;
typedef struct { int sequencing, morality; } face_t;

/* A sparse association from `sequencing` to an int value, growing as needed.
   This is the C rendering of the reference dict: it holds only positions
   actually placed into it, and grows without any fixed ceiling. */
typedef struct { int *sequencing; int *value; int count, cap; } assoc_t;

static void assoc_init(assoc_t *a) { a->sequencing = NULL; a->value = NULL; a->count = 0; a->cap = 0; }
static void assoc_free(assoc_t *a) { free(a->sequencing); free(a->value); }
static int  assoc_find(const assoc_t *a, int seq) {
    for (int i = 0; i < a->count; i++) if (a->sequencing[i] == seq) return i;
    return -1;
}
static void assoc_grow(assoc_t *a) {
    int ncap = a->cap ? a->cap * 2 : 4;
    a->sequencing = (int *)realloc(a->sequencing, (size_t)ncap * sizeof(int));
    a->value      = (int *)realloc(a->value,      (size_t)ncap * sizeof(int));
    a->cap = ncap;
}
/* get with default, exactly like dict.get(seq, dflt) */
static int assoc_get(const assoc_t *a, int seq, int dflt) {
    int i = assoc_find(a, seq); return i < 0 ? dflt : a->value[i];
}
/* set, creating the position if absent */
static void assoc_set(assoc_t *a, int seq, int val) {
    int i = assoc_find(a, seq);
    if (i < 0) { if (a->count == a->cap) assoc_grow(a); i = a->count++; a->sequencing[i] = seq; }
    a->value[i] = val;
}

    s->cap=nc;
}
static void cstore_set(cstore_t *s,int seq,int mor,int comp,int reatt){
    int i=cstore_find(s,seq);
    if(i<0){ if(s->count==s->cap) cstore_grow(s); i=s->count++; s->seq[i]=seq; }
    s->mor[i]=mor; s->comp[i]=comp; s->reatt[i]=reatt;
}

/* bi_coupling. commencing_co_agency: array of carry_t (length ncommencing).
   accepted_bi_morality: array of face_t (length naccepted). bi_attentioning:
   the position-count of the surface. Outputs: bi_moral_sequencing (caller
   provides array of at least bi_attentioning face_t), and the new carry as a
   cstore_t the caller frees. */
void bi_coupling(const carry_t *commencing_co_agency, int ncommencing,
                 const face_t *accepted_bi_morality, int naccepted,
                 int bi_attentioning,
                 face_t *bi_moral_sequencing_out,
                 cstore_t *re_commencing_out) {
    /* offering = {sequencing: competency} from the commencing carry */
    assoc_t offering; assoc_init(&offering);
    for (int i = 0; i < ncommencing; i++)
        assoc_set(&offering, commencing_co_agency[i].sequencing, commencing_co_agency[i].competency);

    /* bi_moral_attentioning: the sparse sign-count. accepted nonzero morality
       counted as-is; commencing carry morality sign-inverted. Only +/-1. */
    assoc_t bi_moral_attentioning; assoc_init(&bi_moral_attentioning);
    for (int i = 0; i < naccepted; i++) {
        int m = accepted_bi_morality[i].morality, s = accepted_bi_morality[i].sequencing;
        if (m > 0) assoc_set(&bi_moral_attentioning, s, assoc_get(&bi_moral_attentioning, s, 0) + 1);
        if (m < 0) assoc_set(&bi_moral_attentioning, s, assoc_get(&bi_moral_attentioning, s, 0) - 1);
    }
    for (int i = 0; i < ncommencing; i++) {
        int m = commencing_co_agency[i].morality, s = commencing_co_agency[i].sequencing;
        if (m > 0) assoc_set(&bi_moral_attentioning, s, assoc_get(&bi_moral_attentioning, s, 0) - 1);
        if (m < 0) assoc_set(&bi_moral_attentioning, s, assoc_get(&bi_moral_attentioning, s, 0) + 1);
    }

    /* bi_moral_sequencing: the surface over the position-count, sign only */
    for (int sequencing = 0; sequencing < bi_attentioning; sequencing++) {
        int t = assoc_get(&bi_moral_attentioning, sequencing, 0);
        bi_moral_sequencing_out[sequencing].sequencing = sequencing;
        bi_moral_sequencing_out[sequencing].morality = (t > 0) ? 1 : (t < 0 ? -1 : 0);
    }

    /* re_commencing_co_agency: persist within the attentioning bound */
    cstore_init(re_commencing_out);
    for (int i = 0; i < ncommencing; i++) {
        int r2 = commencing_co_agency[i].re_attentioning + 1;
        if (r2 <= 3 || (r2 == 4 && commencing_co_agency[i].competency > 0))
            cstore_set(re_commencing_out, commencing_co_agency[i].sequencing,
                       commencing_co_agency[i].morality, commencing_co_agency[i].competency, r2);
    }
    /* fresh surface re-enters the carry, overwriting its position */
    for (int sequencing = 0; sequencing < bi_attentioning; sequencing++) {
        int morality = bi_moral_sequencing_out[sequencing].morality;
        if (morality != 0)
            cstore_set(re_commencing_out, sequencing, morality,
                       0 - assoc_get(&offering, sequencing, 1), 0);
    }

    /* sort the carry by sequencing (insertion sort on the parallel arrays) */
    for (int i = 1; i < re_commencing_out->count; i++) {
        int js = re_commencing_out->seq[i], jm = re_commencing_out->mor[i],
            jc = re_commencing_out->comp[i], jr = re_commencing_out->reatt[i];
        int j = i - 1;
        while (j >= 0 && re_commencing_out->seq[j] > js) {
            re_commencing_out->seq[j+1]   = re_commencing_out->seq[j];
            re_commencing_out->mor[j+1]   = re_commencing_out->mor[j];
            re_commencing_out->comp[j+1]  = re_commencing_out->comp[j];
            re_commencing_out->reatt[j+1] = re_commencing_out->reatt[j];
            j--;
        }
        re_commencing_out->seq[j+1]=js; re_commencing_out->mor[j+1]=jm;
        re_commencing_out->comp[j+1]=jc; re_commencing_out->reatt[j+1]=jr;
    }

    assoc_free(&offering);
    assoc_free(&bi_moral_attentioning);
}
```

```javascript
// Pure port of Exhibit_ONE_Natural_Resolver_v241.py to dynamically-typed,
// JIT-compiled JavaScript. Names follow the reference. The sign-count is a
// Map keyed by `sequencing`, growing only for positions actually sounded, as
// the reference dict does. Increment is only +/-1; only the sign surfaces.
// No fixed range, no bound, no accumulation the reference does not carry.

function biCoupling(commencing_co_agency, accepted_bi_morality, bi_attentioning) {
  // offering = {sequencing: competency}
  const offering = new Map();
  for (const [sequencing, morality, competency, re_attentioning] of commencing_co_agency)
    offering.set(sequencing, competency);

  // bi_inversioning: accepted nonzero morality as-is; carry morality sign-inverted
  const bi_inversioning = [];
  for (const [sequencing, morality] of accepted_bi_morality)
    if (morality !== 0) bi_inversioning.push([sequencing, morality]);
  for (const [sequencing, morality] of commencing_co_agency) {
    if (morality > 0) bi_inversioning.push([sequencing, -1]);
    if (morality < 0) bi_inversioning.push([sequencing, 1]);
  }

  // bi_moral_attentioning: sparse sign-count, only +/-1
  const bi_moral_attentioning = new Map();
  for (const [sequencing, morality] of bi_inversioning) {
    if (morality > 0) bi_moral_attentioning.set(sequencing, (bi_moral_attentioning.get(sequencing) || 0) + 1);
    if (morality < 0) bi_moral_attentioning.set(sequencing, (bi_moral_attentioning.get(sequencing) || 0) - 1);
  }

  // bi_moral_sequencing: surface over the position-count, sign only
  const bi_moral_sequencing = [];
  for (let sequencing = 0; sequencing < bi_attentioning; sequencing++) {
    const t = bi_moral_attentioning.get(sequencing) || 0;
    bi_moral_sequencing.push([sequencing, t > 0 ? 1 : (t < 0 ? -1 : 0)]);
  }

  // re_commencing_co_agency: persist within the attentioning bound
  const re_commencing_co_agency = new Map();
  for (const [sequencing, morality, competency, re_attentioning] of commencing_co_agency) {
    const r2 = re_attentioning + 1;
    if (r2 <= 3 || (r2 === 4 && competency > 0))
      re_commencing_co_agency.set(sequencing, [morality, competency, r2]);
  }
  for (const [sequencing, morality] of bi_moral_sequencing) {
    if (morality !== 0)
      re_commencing_co_agency.set(sequencing, [morality, 0 - (offering.has(sequencing) ? offering.get(sequencing) : 1), 0]);
  }

  const carries = [...re_commencing_co_agency.keys()].sort((a, b) => a - b)
    .map(sequencing => [sequencing, ...re_commencing_co_agency.get(sequencing)]);

  return [bi_moral_sequencing, carries];
}
```

```ocaml
(* Pure port of Exhibit_ONE_Natural_Resolver_v241.py to statically-typed,
   interpreted OCaml. Names follow the reference. The sign-count is an
   association list keyed by [sequencing], holding only positions actually
   sounded and growing as the reference dict does, with no preallocation hint
   and no ceiling. Increment is only +/-1; only the sign surfaces. *)

type carry = { sequencing : int; morality : int; competency : int; re_attentioning : int }
type face  = { sequencing : int; morality : int }

(* sparse association from sequencing to a value, like dict.get(seq, dflt) *)
let assoc_get lst seq dflt =
  match List.assoc_opt seq lst with Some v -> v | None -> dflt

(* set, replacing the position if present, else prepending it *)
let assoc_set lst seq v =
  (seq, v) :: List.remove_assoc seq lst

let bi_coupling
    (commencing_co_agency : carry list)
    (accepted_bi_morality : face list)
    (bi_attentioning : int)
  : face list * carry list =

  (* offering = {sequencing: competency} *)
  let offering =
    List.fold_left (fun acc (cr : carry) -> assoc_set acc cr.sequencing cr.competency)
      [] commencing_co_agency
  in

  (* bi_inversioning: accepted nonzero as-is; carry morality sign-inverted *)
  let bi_inversioning =
    (List.filter_map
       (fun (f : face) -> if f.morality <> 0 then Some (f.sequencing, f.morality) else None)
       accepted_bi_morality)
    @
    (List.filter_map
       (fun (cr : carry) ->
          if cr.morality > 0 then Some (cr.sequencing, -1)
          else if cr.morality < 0 then Some (cr.sequencing, 1) else None)
       commencing_co_agency)
  in

  (* bi_moral_attentioning: sparse sign-count, only +/-1 *)
  let bi_moral_attentioning =
    List.fold_left
      (fun acc (sequencing, morality) ->
         if morality > 0 then assoc_set acc sequencing (assoc_get acc sequencing 0 + 1)
         else if morality < 0 then assoc_set acc sequencing (assoc_get acc sequencing 0 - 1)
         else acc)
      [] bi_inversioning
  in

  (* bi_moral_sequencing: surface over the position-count, sign only *)
  let bi_moral_sequencing =
    List.init bi_attentioning (fun sequencing ->
        let t = assoc_get bi_moral_attentioning sequencing 0 in
        { sequencing; morality = (if t > 0 then 1 else if t < 0 then -1 else 0) })
  in

  (* re_commencing_co_agency: persist within the attentioning bound *)
  let re_commencing =
    List.fold_left
      (fun acc (cr : carry) ->
         let r2 = cr.re_attentioning + 1 in
         if r2 <= 3 || (r2 = 4 && cr.competency > 0)
         then (cr.sequencing, (cr.morality, cr.competency, r2))
              :: List.remove_assoc cr.sequencing acc
         else acc)
      [] commencing_co_agency
  in
  let re_commencing =
    List.fold_left
      (fun acc (f : face) ->
         if f.morality <> 0 then
           let off = assoc_get offering f.sequencing 1 in
           (f.sequencing, (f.morality, 0 - off, 0)) :: List.remove_assoc f.sequencing acc
         else acc)
      re_commencing bi_moral_sequencing
  in

  let carries =
    List.sort (fun (a, _) (b, _) -> compare a b) re_commencing
    |> List.map (fun (sequencing, (morality, competency, re_attentioning)) ->
        { sequencing; morality; competency; re_attentioning })
  in
  (bi_moral_sequencing, carries)
```
