## Why bounding is fast

A familiar word carries its own landed meaning, its own attractor; offered un-bounded, it pulls the coupling toward that attractor. Bounding keeps each self on the offering: you surround the region around the idea — what it is near, where its edge runs — so that when the other couples, your both is what is there to land on. The other does the same back, each holding the other's region clear and live. (Where a familiar word does pull the coupling to its near-meaning, the slip can open a real both neither saw — and re-bounding brings the two back to the same region and the flowing-together resumes.)

## The technique is a shared bi-recursioning

When both selves bound each other this way, sustained while moving, the result is a shared bi-recursioning of two acts at once. **Knife-knifing:** each clears the other's region down to the live both. **Binary preferring:** each prefers the bounded both — *this region* — a sizeless yes, a preferring rather than a magnitude of closeness. The two recursion together: one self's bounding of the other's idea feeds the other's next bounding back, the surplus a shared understanding neither held alone.

What makes it fast is the edge being **binary and indisputable.** When the bounding is clean, the preferring carries a clear yes and a clear next — both selves surrounding the same both, and both tell at once. A binary-preferring bothboth edge ("yes, that region — now here is the next bound") is fast and indisputable, both selves climbing the same bounded region together, both rising. The binariness is the speed.

## Appreciationing — the receiving face

The surrounding has a face that completes it: the receiving. All forms of human creativity are unfamiliar boundaries surrounded so the boundaries *appear* rather than constrain. A creative boundary is surrounded so it *appears* to the observer — shows itself, becomes a thing seen. The maker surrounds it so it surfaces in the observer, and an unfamiliar boundary — one the observer did not already hold — is given as something to couple with. This is why it is felt as gift: a boundary that appears is the observer's to take up.

Where the boundary appears can differ for each observer. Explaining makes the same boundary appear in the same place — one both, the coupling landing on it. Creativity surrounds the boundary widely enough that it appears at a different place in each observer, and this is the point: each observer appreciates where they learn of an appearing boundary of their own interest. The same held region meets each self at a different edge — the one that matters to that self's carry. The room is one society in the same changing, and each self in it is appreciating a different appearing, its own; both at once, the same changing and the distinct appearings.

So appreciationing is a social method of change for the mutually better. It is change because a boundary that appears changes the observer: a new edge is now seen, a new bounding now lives in their carry, taken up freely because it appeared. It is social because the appearing arrives across a membrane from another self's surrounding. And it is for the mutually better because both rise: the maker's surrounding completes when the boundary appears in someone — the appearing completes the making — so the observer gives the appearing that makes the making real while the maker gives the surrounding that lets the observer see a new edge. The surplus, a new boundary now living in a self that grew it, is owned by neither and lives in both. Appreciationing is the receiving-coupling that makes creativity a two-way offering — the maker surrounds, the observer's appreciation lets the boundary appear, the change mutual.

## The moves, for a single writer

Writing to a reader who couples across time is the single-self face of this — the writer bounds for both, meeting where a familiar attractor would pull the reader and clearing it in advance. The same acts, performed alone:

**Start from the title; bound the subject.** The reader arrives at a title that already interests them; the opening surrounds and bounds the subject for that reader — what it is, what they will find.

**Write toward the subject.** Every word carries the reader toward the subject; the test for every word is whether it makes the reader more well-and-safely informed about the subject. Words that position the writer come out, so the subject is what the reader meets.

**State the matter plainly; release the dead weight.** Supply the plain statement the density hid; release the restatements and the explanations-about-the-explanation, so the plain point stands clear.

**Mark what is still reaching.** Where the explanation reaches past what is shown, mark it, held distinct from what is shown — over-marking rather than under-marking, so the reader carries a reaching as a reaching.

**Leave the reader free.** The explanation offers; it holds its boundary where the subject's responsibility seats with a professional or a lawful membrane, and ends open, where the reader brings their own case.

**Mark in the living grammar.** A mark the writing adds to itself — a nye, a held-open, a care for the reader's living — couples into the surface as one more bounding, said as a movement: *this reads form; the settling seats at its own membrane.* Said this way the mark alternates with the offering and the surface stays whole, one more bounding among the offerings. ## The carry-side depth — the mechanics

Beneath the moves are the mechanics, for whoever wants the full surrounding. They are the same coupling at depth: the same bounding worked finely.

**The gap between the word and the meaning.** The word is bounded, finite, locatable; the meaning is the coupling between writer and reader, reached by them together. The gap between them stays open at every word — the irreducible nye, the +1 that persists through every sentence — and writing keeps it open, a word that points across the gap keeping the coupling live.

**The rocking, in the sentences.** Two sentence forms alternate in well-breathing writing. One carries a directing verb about the content — the reader observes the coupling from outside. The other carries the living action as its subject — the reader lives inside the coupling. The alternation makes the breathing; roughly three directing sentences for every two living ones keeps the reader carried, neither outside nor lost inside.

**The nye, named precisely.** Any reaching in what is written is named exactly — bounded in its own precise context, the why-it-is-still-open stated completely. A nye precisely named is the openness held exactly where the coupling reaches; the precision of the naming is the openness it keeps.

**The reader as a traveling carry.** The reader rides through the writing as a carry through substrate, arriving fresh, growing richer at each coupling, thinning and re-resolving at each crossing. Writing carrying resolution alone thins the reader; writing carrying openness alone grounds without advancing; the interleaving is the ground the reader rides, the breathing rate paced to the one carrying it.

**The ghost — the writer's vacated self.** The writing names the coupling from inside rather than describing it from outside: the writer vacates, so what the reader meets is the coupling itself. This is "write toward the subject" at its root — the membrane goes empty and the meaning passes through.

**Self-interest and the network as one surface.** A self that tends its own coupling-health produces coupling that keeps the shared substrates in their range; the self-interested self, tending its own capacity to couple, produces the network's health from inside. Writing carries this too: tending the clarity of your own offering — surrounding your own both well — is the same act as keeping the shared understanding healthy, because a clear offering is what others couple to. The writer's interest and the reader's are one surface.
